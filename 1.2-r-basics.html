Introduction to Statistics and Computation with Data

1.2 R basics

1.2.1 The c() function

Suppose we wish to enter Scores in Computer Science class of 10 students. The scores are 40, 39, 15, 6, 18, 22, 30, 21, 15, 23.

Scores <- c(40, 39, 15, 6, 18, 22, 30, 21, 15, 23)

We have assigned values to a variable Scores with the assignment operator <-. = can also be used for assignment but throughout this book we will use R-community preferred one ,i.e., <-. The value don’t get automatically displayed unless we call the variable.

Scores
##  [1] 40 39 15  6 18 22 30 21 15 23

1.2.2 Inbuilt functions

R has many inbuilt functions

(40 + 39 + 15 + 6 + 18 + 22 + 30 + 21 + 15 + 23) / 10
## [1] 22.9

Instead of writing such manaul expression we can just use the mean() function.

mean(Scores)
## [1] 22.9

To calculate standard deviation you can use sd() function

sd(Scores)
## [1] 10.75433

1.2.3 Slicing a vector

Suppose we want to change the entry of student 4 from 6 to 16 in Scores.

Scores2 <- Scores
Scores2[4] <- 16
Scores2
##  [1] 40 39 15 16 18 22 30 21 15 23

Selecting some entries of Scores2

Scores[c(1, 3, 5)]
## [1] 40 15 18

Suppose we want to see which students’ Scores are equal to 30 marks

which(Scores == 30)
## [1] 7

or those with Scores lesser than or equal to 20.

which(Scores <= 20)
## [1] 3 4 5 9
